## Overview

This skill helps create engaging LinkedIn posts that announce research achievements, conference presentations, and academic milestones using VLCMatrix Lab's successful post format.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user wants to:
- Create a LinkedIn post about a research paper or presentation
- Announce a conference acceptance or presentation
- Share lab achievements or milestones
- Analyze the structure of successful LinkedIn posts
- Improve or rewrite an existing LinkedIn draft
- Generate multiple post variations

## How to Use This Skill

### Step 1: Load Reference Examples

Always start by reading the example posts to understand the style:
- Read `references/example-posts.md` to see successful post patterns

### Step 2: Gather Required Information

Collect the following details from the user (ask if not provided):

**Essential:**
- Research title / paper name
- Researcher name(s)
- Supervisor name
- Conference name and date
- Brief description of the research
- Key problem being solved

**Optional but Recommended:**
- Specific achievements (metrics, percentages, results)
- Real-world applications
- Dataset names
- Technical challenges addressed
- Team members to acknowledge

### Step 3: Generate the Post

Follow this proven structure:

1. **Opening Headline** (Bold)
   - Format: "[Lab Name] [Action Verb] [Focus Area]"
   - Examples: "VLCMatrix Lab Showcasing Research Excellence in AI and Computer Vision"
   - Use strong action verbs: Showcasing, Leading, Advancing, Pioneering

2. **Introduction**
   - Start with: "We are delighted to share..." or "We are proud to highlight..."
   - Mention it's another achievement if applicable
   - Include researcher name and supervisor

3. **Research Title** (Quoted)
   - Present the exact paper title in quotes
   - Make it stand out on its own line

4. **Conference Details**
   - Format: "Presented at [Conference Name]"
   - Include date on next line

5. **Research Description** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - First paragraph: What problem does it solve? What's the approach?
   - Second paragraph: Technical details and methodology
   - Use technical language but keep it accessible

6. **Key Achievements** (Optional - use when metrics available)
   - Use checkmarks: ✅
   - List 3-5 concrete results
   - Include percentages, metrics, or specific outcomes
   - Format: "✅ [Metric/Achievement]"

7. **Impact Statement**
   - Start with: "By integrating..." or "This research advances..."
   - Connect to real-world applications
   - Mention practical use cases
   - End with broader impact

8. **Closing Congratulations**
   - Thank and congratulate the team
   - Mention specific names
   - Acknowledge the contribution

9. **Hashtags**
   - Include 10-15 relevant hashtags
   - Start with lab/brand hashtag (#VLCMatrixLab)
   - Add technical hashtags (#AIResearch #ComputerVision #MachineLearning)
   - Include domain-specific tags
   - Add conference hashtag (#ConferenceName2025)
   - Format: #CamelCase for multi-word tags

### Step 4: Tone and Style Guidelines

**Tone:**
- Professional and academic
- Proud but humble
- Enthusiastic about impact
- Technically accurate

**Style:**
- Use active voice
- Keep paragraphs concise (2-4 sentences)
- Emphasize real-world applications
- Balance technical depth with accessibility
- Use specific metrics when available

**Formatting:**
- Bold the opening headline
- Use line breaks for readability
- Separate sections with blank lines
- Use checkmarks (✅) for achievements
- Quote the research title

### Step 5: Variations and Customization

**For Different Research Types:**
- **AI/ML Research:** Emphasize datasets, accuracy metrics, novel architectures
- **Engineering/Safety:** Focus on practical applications, safety improvements
- **Interdisciplinary Work:** Highlight the combination of fields
- **Applied Research:** Stress real-world deployment and impact

**Adjust Length:**
- **Short version:** Skip "Key Achievements" section, condense to 1 paragraph
- **Standard version:** Follow full structure (200-300 words)
- **Extended version:** Add more technical details, expand applications

## Common Variations

### Variation 1: Conference Acceptance (Before Presentation)
- Use: "We are excited to announce..."
- Change: "Presented at" → "Accepted at" or "To be presented at"
- Focus on significance of acceptance

### Variation 2: Journal Publication
- Change: Conference details → Journal name and volume
- Add: Impact factor or journal ranking if prestigious
- Mention: Publication date or "now available"

### Variation 3: Multiple Papers/Achievements
- Group related achievements
- Use numbered or bulleted structure
- Keep individual descriptions brief

### Variation 4: Collaborative Research
- Emphasize partnership organizations
- Mention all contributing institutions
- Highlight collaborative aspects in impact statement

## Tips for High Engagement

1. **Lead with Impact:** Open with the most impressive aspect
2. **Use Numbers:** Metrics and percentages increase credibility
3. **Tag People:** Mention and tag team members (if applicable)
4. **Timing:** Post during business hours (9 AM - 3 PM local time)
5. **Visual:** Suggest adding conference photos, diagrams, or lab images
6. **Call to Action:** Can end with "Read the full paper at [link]" if available

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- ✅ All names spelled correctly
- ✅ Conference name and date accurate
- ✅ Research title in quotes
- ✅ Supervisor clearly mentioned
- ✅ At least one real-world application mentioned
- ✅ 10-15 relevant hashtags included
- ✅ Tone is professional yet enthusiastic
- ✅ Post length is 200-400 words (optimal for LinkedIn)
- ✅ Line breaks for readability
- ✅ No typos or grammatical errors
